## 2. Expert Empires

**What they are:** UK's leading events and mastermind company for "expert" businesses. Founded by Nick James. Tagline: "The Home for Expert Businesses."

**What they sell:** Business coaching, masterminds, and live events. Four-tier mastermind ladder from 100K Mastermind to Inner Circle. Entry products include GBP 1 Marketing Accelerator Days and GBP 1,995 Mastermind Intensives.

**ICP:** Coaches, consultants, speakers, trainers, authors. Primarily UK-based.

**What they do NOT do:** They are not an agency. They don't run ads for clients. They teach frameworks. They even outsource their own paid traffic to an external agency (Impactful Marketing).

**Revenue model:** Recurring mastermind memberships + event tickets + one-off intensives.

### Conflict Analysis

**No conflict on service type:** Expert Empires sells education/coaching. Jossiah sells done-for-you ad management. Completely different value propositions. Expert Empires doesn't even have deep in-house paid traffic capabilities.

**Potential perception conflict on ICP:** Both target coaches/consultants/speakers. Even though the services don't overlap, targeting the same people could feel like poaching from the community Jossiah helped build.

**What Jossiah should avoid:**
- Don't publicly recruit from the Expert Empires community or Facebook group
- Don't position against Expert Empires or imply their marketing training is inadequate
- Don't use Expert Empires case studies, internal data, or proprietary frameworks
- Don't use language like "you don't need a mastermind, you need an agency"

**Strategic opportunity:** Expert Empires members are taught marketing strategy but lack execution capability. Jossiah could be the recommended vendor Nick sends members to when they say "I understand the strategy but need someone to run my ads." Complementary, not competitive.

## 3. Elite Closing Academy

**What they are:** Sales training company co-founded by Matt Elwell and Nick James in 2018. IAPC&M accredited.

**What they sell:** Sales training for business owners and sales teams. Core framework: "Serve > Sell > Close." Products range from GBP 4.95 "Cold to Sold" book to 3-day certification courses to GBP 297/month Skool community.

**Claims:** GBP 26M+ created for clients, 2,800+ businesses trained, 19 countries, 100+ industries.

**ICP:** Service-based business owners uncomfortable with sales, coaches, consultants, agency owners, corporate sales teams.

**Critical positioning distinction:** ECA explicitly states they are "NOT about getting you more sales through increasing marketing budgets and lead flow!" They teach closing. They do not generate leads.

### Conflict Analysis

**Fundamentally complementary:** ECA teaches people to close. Jossiah generates the leads to close. These are different parts of the funnel.

**What Jossiah should avoid:**
- Don't use "closing" language (close more deals, increase close rate)
- Don't position as a sales trainer or coach
- Don't use "Serve, Sell, Close" or any ECA framework language
- Don't offer sales process advice as part of his service
- Avoid "without being pushy/sleazy" - this is core ECA positioning
- Stay firmly in the "paid traffic / lead generation" lane

**Clean framing:** "I get the leads. Your job is to close them."

## 5. Market Frustrations

### Top 10 Complaints (ranked by frequency)

1. **Zero results despite significant spend.** "Dozens of business owners spent $10K, $25K, even $100K on agencies that simply didn't deliver."

2. **Vanity metrics instead of real outcomes.** "Impressive-looking reports filled with impressions and engagement rates, but sales remain flat." "73% of marketing budgets can't be directly linked to business outcomes."

3. **Bait-and-switch on talent.** Senior team pitches, junior team delivers. "So many do this and it's incredibly irritating."

4. **Don't understand the client's niche.** Agencies "conflated learning designers with HR generalists, missing crucial distinctions." One-size-fits-all strategies "that could apply to a dentist, SaaS brand, clothing store, and crypto newsletter with only the logo changed."

5. **Black box operations.** "Hand over thousands each month to an agency that never quite explains what's happening." Reports designed to obscure, not inform.

6. **Contract lock-ins / data hostage.** "The agency owns your ad accounts, your landing pages, your creative assets, your email lists. You're held hostage." Cancellation fees of 50-100% of remaining contract value.

7. **Set-and-forget.** "Start strong then phone it in after month two." No ongoing optimization.

8. **Poor communication.** "The responsive salesperson who courted you disappears after signing."

9. **Broken retainer model.** "You're not paying for marketing. You're paying rent." Agencies stretch simple tasks because "profitability depends on hours billed, not results delivered."

10. **Over-promising, under-delivering.** Guaranteed ROAS claims. "Double your sales in 30 days."

### What They Wish They Had

- Results-tied compensation / skin in the game
- Industry-specific expertise (someone who already understands coaching/consulting)
- A single accountable person
- Month-to-month flexibility
- Full account ownership from day one
- Plain-language communication and reporting
- Strategic thinking, not just execution
- Education alongside execution (build their understanding, not dependency)

### The Buying Journey

1. **Problem recognition:** Revenue plateaus, referrals dry up, competitor growing visibly
2. **Research:** Ask in peer communities, Google, consume agency content (3-5 options minimum, 10-15 touchpoints before deciding)
3. **Evaluation:** Proof with similar businesses > transparent pricing > niche expertise > communication quality > contract flexibility > account ownership > who they'll actually work with
4. **Decision:** 2-4 weeks from first contact. Final trigger is usually a call where they feel genuinely understood. "Do I trust this person?"

### Pricing Expectations (Buyer Side)

- Management fees: GBP 500-3,000/month (most expect GBP 1,000-2,000)
- Minimum ad spend: GBP 1,000-3,000/month
- Total monthly: GBP 2,000-5,000 all-in
- Expect meaningful results within 60-90 days
- After 90 days of no results, most will fire the agency
- Preferred model: flat monthly fee or hybrid (base + performance bonus)
- Most distrusted model: percentage of ad spend ("incentivizes higher budgets regardless of efficiency")

## 6. Implications for the Site

### What must change

1. **Case study numbers are fabricated.** No public evidence supports any of the specific numbers on the current site. They need to be removed entirely, not softened.

2. **Founder stats are unverifiable.** "GBP 3.2M spend managed," "7yr running ads," "40+ B2B accounts" - none of these can be verified from public sources. Restructure the about section around what IS real: his background, his dual media-buying-and-sales experience, and his immersion in the coaching/consulting world via Expert Empires and ECA.

3. **The site reads as generic-good.** It could be any agency. Nothing about it is unmistakably Jossiah's. The research shows his differentiator is: one person, who understands the expert/coaching world from the inside, who does both media buying AND understands sales (from ECA).

4. **Missing trust signals.** The market screams for: account ownership, no contracts, transparent pricing, single point of contact. These should be front and center, not buried in FAQ.

5. **FAQ needs real objections.** The current FAQ is fine but misses the deepest buyer fears: "will you actually run my account or hand it off?", "do I own my ad accounts?", "what happens when I want to leave?"

### What should stay

- The brand system (navy + mint, mono tags, chart aesthetic) is strong and differentiated
- The locked copy elements (hero headline, eyebrow, CTA) are good
- The section structure (method, about, FAQ, CTA) works
- The single-operator positioning is the strongest differentiator and aligns perfectly with market demand

### Positioning sweet spot

The research points to one clear positioning: **The anti-agency.** One person. Your accounts. No contracts. No dashboards you'll never log into. This isn't a positioning choice - it's literally what the market is begging for, and it's what Jossiah actually is.
