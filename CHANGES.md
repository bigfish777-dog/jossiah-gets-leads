# Changes Log

Every change made to the site, with reasoning.

---

## Section 2: Cases -> "How it works" (transparency section)

**What changed:** Removed the three fake case study cards entirely. Replaced with a "How it works" section covering: account ownership, weekly reporting, and 30-day rolling contracts.

**Why:** Research found zero verifiable results attributable to Jossiah personally. The case study numbers (61% CPL reduction, 3.2x pipeline, GBP 11 booked calls) were fabricated in the design prototype. Publishing unverifiable numbers for a brand-new agency would destroy credibility with anyone who checks. Meanwhile, market research showed that account ownership, transparent reporting, and no lock-in contracts are the three things B2B buyers most desperately want and most agencies refuse to offer. This section now addresses real buyer anxieties instead of making unprovable claims.

**Alternative considered:** A "case studies coming Q3" holding section. Rejected because it draws attention to the absence of social proof rather than redirecting to genuine differentiators. The trust signals in "How it works" do more work than an empty promise.

## Section 3: About - removed unverifiable stats

**What changed:** Removed the stats bar (GBP 3.2M spend managed, 7yr running ads, 40+ B2B accounts). Rewrote the about copy to focus on Jossiah's actual differentiator: in-house media buyer for coaching/sales training businesses who also understands sales because he's done it.

**Why:** None of the stats could be verified from public sources. His LinkedIn doesn't specify dates that would confirm 7 years. No public record of GBP 3.2M in managed spend. Making up numbers for a personal brand site is a liability - if a prospect asks "which 40 accounts?" there's no answer. The rewritten copy uses what IS true: he's been media buying for Expert Empires and Elite Closing Academy (verifiable from their team pages), and he has sales experience (his ECA title is Sales Executive). The dual media-buyer-plus-sales angle is genuinely unusual and more compelling than generic stats.

**Alternative considered:** Keeping the stats but softening them ("multiple six figures in spend managed"). Rejected because vague stats are worse than no stats - they signal the person wanted to claim numbers but couldn't back them up.

## Method section - "we" to "I" voice shift

**What changed:** Changed "We sell you the three courses" to "I sell you" and "We start where everyone else finishes" to "I start." Kept the locked hero "We run the ads" unchanged.

**Why:** It's a one-person agency. The method section describes Jossiah's personal approach. Using "we" when there's demonstrably one person feels dishonest. The hero headline "We run the ads" works because it's a brand voice speaking, but the method descriptions are personal and should sound personal.

## Method card copy refinements

**What changed:** Minor copy tightening. "No 'let's test 47 audiences' until the offer's clear" became "If the offer doesn't land, no amount of targeting will fix it." Second card: added "and 'leads' that never pick up" to the existing copy.

**Why:** The original copy was good but slightly too clever. "47 audiences" is a specific reference that not every prospect will get. The replacement is more direct and universally understood. The addition to card 2 targets a specific pain point from market research: agencies delivering "leads" that are just form fills from people who won't pick up the phone.

---

## New section: Fit check

**What changed:** Added a "This is probably not for you" section between About and FAQ. Two-column layout: good fit vs. not a fit, with specific qualifying/disqualifying criteria.

**Why:** Three reasons from research:
1. The best agencies in the space use qualification sections to build trust through exclusivity - saying "no" to some people makes the "yes" more valuable.
2. Market research shows prospects are tired of agencies that take anyone with a credit card. Demonstrating selectivity signals confidence.
3. Practically, it pre-qualifies leads so Jossiah doesn't waste calls on pre-revenue founders who can't afford the minimum spend. The specific numbers (GBP 1,000+ per client, GBP 3,500+/month total) set expectations before the call.

**Alternative considered:** A "who this is for" section (positive only). Rejected because the "not for you" framing is psychologically more effective - it creates contrast and makes the reader self-select. The people who ARE a fit read the "not a fit" column and think "that's not me, I'm serious."

---

## FAQ overhaul

**What changed:** Expanded from 6 to 8 questions. Reordered to lead with the questions that address the biggest market frustrations (who runs my account, do I own my data). Added questions about reports and account ownership. Strengthened existing answers with more specific commitments.

**Why:** Market research identified the top buyer anxieties: bait-and-switch on talent, data hostage situations, black-box reporting, over-promising. The new FAQ directly addresses each of these. Leading with "Who actually runs my account?" mirrors the #3 complaint (senior pitches, junior delivers). "Do I own my ad accounts?" mirrors #6 (data hostage). The report question mirrors #5 (black box). Each answer makes a specific, concrete commitment rather than a vague reassurance.
